About this manual

This manual is the canonical reference for everyone on the Penn Home Medical Supply customer service team. It walks you through every screen of the PennPaps admin console, every customer-facing flow on the storefront, and the policies behind both. Read it in order on day one; keep it on your second monitor after that.

PennPaps is built on a single guiding principle: patients get answers in seconds, not days. Every workflow in this document is designed so you can resolve a question on the first contact — whether it arrives by phone, SMS, email, web chat, or a review left on a product page.

How to use this manual. Each chapter opens with a screenshot of the screen in question, followed by the data on it, the actions available, and the customer-facing language we use to describe it. If a section is marked PHI the contents are protected health information — never paste them into a public channel or screenshot to a personal device.

Chapter 1

Platform overview

1.1 · The four jobs PennPaps does

PennPaps is one product with four tightly-integrated jobs. Knowing which "job" a customer is in is the single fastest way to solve their problem.

Job Who uses it What it does Where it lives
Mask fitter New patients On-device facial measurement & clinical questionnaire produce a ranked mask recommendation. /how-it-works → /capture → /measure → /questionnaire → /results
Cash-pay shop Returning & uninsured patients Direct-to-consumer storefront with Stripe Hosted Checkout, wishlists, and Subscribe & Save. /shop, /shop/cart, /shop/checkout, /account
Insurance pipeline Insured patients Qualifier form captures insurance + Rx info; leads route to the admin team for verification. /insurance → admin Insurance Leads

All four jobs ship as one customer-facing site (pennfit.up.railway.app) and one internal admin console mounted at /admin/*. There is no separate dashboard URL to remember and no second SPA to load — whatever a customer sees, you can see (and act on) from the same domain.

1.2 · Why we are the best CPAP supply app on the market

Customers compare us to legacy DME suppliers and to one-stop online shops. We win on every axis. Memorize these — they are the differentiators you should bring up unprompted on every call.

1. The on-device fitter. Most competitors guess mask sizing from a photo upload — we measure facial landmarks on the patient's own phone using MediaPipe Face Mesh and send only numbers. The patient's image never leaves the browser, never touches our servers, and is never logged.
2. One unified surface. Fitter, shop, insurance, and resupply all live behind the same login. Patients are not shipped between three different vendors with three different accounts. Reps see the whole journey in one Patient 360 view.
3. Two-way conversational AI. Patients can text us in plain English ("running low on supplies, ship me what worked last time") and the system threads it into the right episode. Voice calls use OpenAI's voice API for natural interactions; SMS uses GPT-completion drafts that you review before sending.
4. One-tap reorder. Past orders are stored on the customer's account (encrypted at rest in Postgres). A patient who ordered twelve months ago can reorder the exact same SKU and size with a single tap on their phone.
5. Subscribe & Save with full transparency. Recurring orders driven by Stripe with the customer in full control — pause, skip, or change cadence from the account page without calling us. (You can do the same from the admin Subscriptions page when they ask you to.)
6. Insurance done right. The qualifier is a real form — not a 'we'll get back to you' explainer page. Patients fill in their carrier and member ID; we route the lead to verification inside the admin Insurance Leads queue and ship as soon as eligibility comes back clean.

1.3 · Privacy & the on-device promise

Two non-negotiable rules govern everything we do with patient data — repeat them to any patient who asks how we keep them safe.

  • Camera images and video frames never leave the browser. Only numeric facial measurements are transmitted. No image is ever logged, persisted, or visible to staff.
  • Order request bodies are not logged. Treat every log line as world-readable; PHI lives only in the application database, behind row-level access controls.
You will be asked. Patients ask "where do my photos go?" on roughly one in eight calls. The answer is: nowhere. The scan happens entirely on their device using a public on-device machine-learning model (MediaPipe Face Mesh). The 16 numeric measurements we keep — bridge width, philtrum depth, etc. — are the only thing transmitted to us, and they are encrypted in transit.
Chapter 2

The customer journey

2.1 · Storefront & mask fitter

Patients arrive at pennfit.up.railway.app from search, sleep-study referrals, and our doctor partner network. The home page gives them three doors: start the fitter, browse the shop, or check insurance.

The fitter is a five-step flow. Customers can pause and resume; their session is anonymous until they create an account.

  1. Consent (/consent). Plain-language overview of what we measure and what we do not store. Patients must explicitly accept before the camera turns on.
  2. Capture (/capture). Front-camera live preview with on-screen alignment guides. MediaPipe Face Mesh runs locally; the page only proceeds once landmarks are stable for two consecutive seconds.
  3. Measure (/measure). The 16 numeric measurements are computed on-device and sent to the API as a JSON payload. The image is discarded.
  4. Questionnaire (/questionnaire). Eight clinical questions: side sleeper, mouth-breather, claustrophobia, facial hair, glasses, etc. Each maps to mask attributes in the catalog.
  5. Results (/results). Top three masks are shown, ranked by a weighted scoring formula that combines fit (measurements) and feel (questionnaire). Patients see a confidence band, not a single "answer".

2.2 · Insurance qualifier vs. cash-pay

Customers who click Order through insurance land on the Insurance qualifier (/insurance). It is a real form: carrier, member ID, group number, primary cardholder, optional Rx upload. Submissions create a row in insurance_leads and appear in the Insurance Leads queue inside the admin console (Chapter 4.4).

Cash-pay customers go straight to a Stripe Hosted Checkout session — Stripe is the source of truth for products and prices, so the admin Inventory page (Chapter 4.4) reflects whatever you change in the Stripe dashboard within seconds.

Decision Cash-pay Insurance
Time to ship Same / next business day 2–5 days (after eligibility)
Out-of-pocket Listed price Co-pay + deductible only
Rx required No Yes (we can request it)
Ships through Stripe Checkout → fulfillment Insurance Leads queue → fulfillment
Patient sees in admin as Customer 360 Patient 360 + Insurance Lead

2.3 · Customer accounts & one-tap reorder

Signed-in customers have an Account hub at /account showing past orders, payment methods (the last four digits — full cards live with Stripe), shipping addresses, active subscriptions, and saved wishlists. The standout feature is one-tap reorder: every prior order has a "Order again" button that re-creates the cart with the same SKU and size in one tap.

Why this matters on a call. When a patient says "send me what I had last time", you can do it from the Customers → Customer Detail page in three clicks. They never have to remember the model name. (Chapter 4.2.)

3.4 · Patients (Patient 360)

The Patients page lists every PHI-bearing patient in the system — distinct from the Customers page (cash shoppers without an open insurance lead). Click into a row to see the full Patient 360: demographics, prescription, mask history, every conversation, every episode, and a notes timeline.

  • Phone & email lookup across all touchpoints — one search box, every channel.
  • Status: active, paused, churned. Pausing suppresses outbound automation without deleting data.
  • Notes: internal-only timeline; never visible to the patient.
  • Recent activity: last fitter run, last order, last reply — at a glance.
PHI handling. Don't paste patient names, phone numbers, prescriptions, or carrier IDs into Slack, email, screenshot tools, or anything not behind PennPaps SSO. The audit log records every page view that surfaces PHI; pasting it elsewhere will not.

3.5 · Follow-ups, canned replies, macros

Three small but heavily-used tools:

  • Follow-ups (/admin/followups) — your personal "come back to this" queue. Snooze a conversation and it lands here at the chosen time. Also surfaces every conversation assigned to you that has been silent for 24 hours.
  • Canned Replies / Macros (/admin/macros) — shared, branded message templates with variable interpolation ({{first_name}}, {{mask_model}}, {{rma_link}}). Use these instead of free-typing common responses.
  • Rule Tester (/admin/rule-tester) — sandbox for the rule engine. Operators (not agents) use it to dry-run a rule against a fake patient before flipping it on.

4.2 · Customers (Customer 360)

Customers (/admin/shop/customers) is the cash-pay analogue of Patients. Lifetime value, every order, every subscription, every abandoned cart, every review they've left, every wishlist they've saved — all on one screen. The Reorder for customer action lives here too: click any past order and the system creates a fresh cart and emails them a checkout link.

4.3 · Abandoned carts & back-in-stock

The shop captures two recoverable signals:

  • Abandoned Carts (/admin/shop/abandoned-carts) — a customer added a product but didn't finish checkout. After 4 hours we send a reminder email; after 24 hours, a coupon. You can re-trigger either from this page.
  • Back-in-Stock (/admin/shop/back-in-stock) — customers who asked to be notified when a SKU returns. The page shows pending notifications grouped by product so you can prioritize what to re-stock.

4.4 · Reviews, Q&A, inventory, insurance leads

  • Reviews (/admin/shop/reviews) — moderation queue for product reviews. Approve, reply publicly, or hide. Replies email the reviewer through the shared SendGrid client (one From address: info@pennpaps.com).
  • Product Q&A (/admin/shop/product-questions) — customer-asked questions on product pages. You answer here; the answer publishes on the storefront and emails the asker.
  • Inventory (/admin/shop/inventory) — read-side mirror of Stripe's product/price catalog. Stripe is the source of truth; this page is the team's view of it.
  • Insurance Leads (/admin/shop/insurance-leads) — every qualifier-form submission. Triage, run eligibility, and convert to a fulfilled order from here. The detail page links the lead, the patient, and any existing conversations.
Chapter 5

Insights, system, and audit

5.1 · Operations & delivery failures

Two pages exist specifically for the things that go wrong, so you can find them before the patient calls.

  • Operations (/admin/operations) — health board: SMS delivery rate, email open/bounce rate, voice answer rate, pg-boss queue depth, last reminder scan, last PHI sweep. If anything is red here, escalate to the on-call lead before promising a delivery time.
  • Delivery Failures (/admin/delivery-failures) — bounced emails, undeliverable SMS, voicemails-only after three attempts. Each row has a "switch channel" button so you can move the patient onto a working address.

5.2 · Reports & storefront analytics

Four reporting surfaces, in order of how often you'll use them:

  1. Reports — operational metrics (resolution time, first-response time, fulfillment lead time, churn) by week/month/agent.
  2. Storefront Analytics — funnel: home → fitter → results → cart → checkout → confirmed. Use this to spot drop-off when product changes ship.
  3. Reminders — outbound reminder cadence health: how many fired, how many converted, how many bounced.
  4. Storefront Audit — a simplified audit view scoped to shop activity (separate from the full system audit log).

5.3 · Rules, rule tester, audit logs

Operators (not agents) own these screens; they're documented here so you understand what's driving your inbox.

  • Rules — every automation in the system: when to send the first resupply nudge, when to escalate to a phone call, when to move a conversation to awaiting_admin. Listed with a short summary; tap to expand the conditional.
  • Rule Tester — paste a fake event, see which rules would fire and what each rule would do. Used before rolling a rule change to production.
  • Audit Log — every admin action recorded with operator, target, timestamp, and IP. PHI never appears in the log — only the IDs and the action verbs.
  • Settings & Team — admin-only. Invite, promote, demote, and remove team members; rotate the link-signing HMAC if a leak is suspected.
If a customer asks "did someone change my order?" the audit log can tell you who did what and when. From the order detail, click the timestamp on the activity entry — it deep-links into the audit log filtered to that record. This is the single most powerful feature for resolving "I didn't authorize that" disputes in under a minute.
Chapter 6

Day-in-the-life playbook

6.1 · Common scenarios & how to solve them

Scenario A · "My mask is leaking"

  1. Open Conversations → click into the patient. Patient 360 shows current mask SKU + size.
  2. Confirm sizing in the questionnaire history. If the patient has gained / lost weight or grown a beard, re-run the fitter (Send fitter link macro).
  3. If sizing was wrong: open RMA, ship the next size up under Comfort Guarantee (no charge to patient).
  4. If sizing was right: ship a fresh cushion (resupply episode) and check that the headgear isn't over-tightened.

Scenario B · "Pause my subscription"

  1. Customers → search by name or phone → click their profile.
  2. Scroll to Subscriptions → click Pause. Choose duration (4, 8, 12 weeks) or "until I unpause".
  3. Stripe pauses the recurring charge automatically. The next-ship-date moves; the patient gets an email.

Scenario C · "I never got my order"

  1. Look up the order in /admin/pennpaps/orders. Check the tracking link.
  2. If the carrier shows delivered but the patient says no: open Delivery Failures to see if there's a known issue at that ZIP.
  3. Reship same SKU at no charge; if the original turns up, the patient keeps it (under $10 in cost — not worth the friction).
  4. For high-value items, escalate to a lead before reshipping.

Scenario D · "Insurance denied my claim"

  1. Open the patient's Insurance Lead (linked from Patient 360).
  2. Check the denial reason. Most common: missing recent sleep study or expired Rx — both can be re-submitted.
  3. Send the patient the Rx-upload link macro; once received, re-run eligibility from the lead detail page.
  4. If we can't qualify them, offer cash-pay with the loyalty discount macro.
